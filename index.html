LET'S TALK MENTAL HEALTH

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also determines how we handle stress, relate to others and make choices.

EARLY WARNING SIGNS

1. Eating or sleeping too
2. Pulling away from people and usual activities.
3. Having unexplained ache and pain.
4. Feeling numb.
5. Low or no energy.
6. Feeling hopeless or helpless.
7. Having thoughts and memories you cant get out of your head.
8. Hearing voices or believing thigs that are not true.
9. Feeling unusually confused, forgetful, on edge, angry, upset,worried, or scared.
10. Thinking of harming yourself.

MENTAL HEALTH WELLNESS

There are various methods for managing mental health problems. Treatment is highly individual, and what works for one person may not work for another.

Treatments include;

Psychotherapists, or talking theerapists

This type of treatment takes a psychological approach or treating illness. Congnitive behavioral therapy, exposure therapy and dialectal behaviour therapy are examples.

Psychiatrists, psychologist, psychotherapists and some primary care physicians carry out this type of treatment. It can help people understand the root of their mental illness and start to work on more healthful thought patterns that support everyday living and reduce the risk of isolation.

Medication

Some people take prescribed medications, such as anti-depressants, anti-psychotics and anxiotic drugs. Although these canâ€™t cure mental disorders, some medications can improve symptoms and help a person resume social interractions and a normal routine while they work on their mental health.

Self help

A person coping with mental health difficulties will usually need to make changes to their lifestyle to facilitate wellness. Such changes might include reducing alcohol intake, sleeping more, and eaing a balanced nutritious diet. People may take time away from work or resolve issues with personal relationships that may be causing damage to their mental health. People with conditions such as anxiety or depressive disorder may benefit from relaxation techniques, wich include deep breathing, meditation and mindfulness.
